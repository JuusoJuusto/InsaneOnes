# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Minecraft players (Java Edition, premium accounts) hunting for a chaotic,
unstable SMP. They arrive skeptical: they have seen dead servers, pay-to-win
"anarchy" servers, and laggy clones. Their context is a browser tab open next to
their Minecraft launcher. The job to be done in one visit: understand what
InsaneOnes is, trust that it is alive and genuinely lawless, copy the IP, and
join. A large share also want to land in the Discord first.

## Product Purpose

InsaneOnes is an unstable survival SMP: chaotic and lawless, no map resets, no
admin interference, a player-run economy with a shop, and cross-version play (the
world runs on 26.1.2 but players join from almost any version). It is NOT
hardcore (players respawn, dropping their inventory) and NOT pay-to-win. The
website is the front door. Success is a
player copying `mc.insaneones.xyz`, joining the world, or hopping into Discord,
without ever doubting the server is real and online. Secondary success: the
owner can change the IP, Discord link, version, and copy from one config file
without touching layout or design.

## Brand Personality

Lawless, ominous, unfiltered. Voice is blunt and a little menacing, never
corporate, never cheerful onboarding-speak: "There are no rules. Only survival."
Three words: brutal, end-game, honest. The honesty matters: anarchy players
respect a server that states plainly what it is (you will be killed, griefed,
and betrayed, and that is the point) over one that oversells safety. Emotional
goal: the dread-tinged thrill of stepping into a world with no safety net.

## Anti-references

- Generic "gaming" templates: rainbow RGB gradients, glassmorphism cards,
  hexagon meshes, esports-team chrome.
- Pay-to-win server sites that lead with a store, ranks, and crate keys.
- Cheerful Hypixel-style minigame-hub friendliness (mascots, pastel, rounded
  bubbly everything). InsaneOnes is the opposite of a theme park.
- Cheap Minecraft cliche: wall-to-wall pixel/blocky fonts used as the body
  typeface. A pixel nod is fine as a small accent; pixel-as-bodytext reads cheap.
- AI brutalist tell: a single Archivo-Black uppercase headline on flat black
  with no atmosphere, depth, or motion. Character must come from art direction.

## Design Principles

- **State what it is.** Lead with the truth of anarchy (no rules, no mercy), not
  with a feature list. The honesty is the marketing.
- **Prove it is alive.** A live player count and status, fetched in real time,
  does more for trust than any adjective. Show, do not claim.
- **One job per fold.** Long scroll, deliberate pacing, one dominant idea per
  section. The whole funnel points at "copy IP / join Discord."
- **Atmosphere over decoration.** The dread comes from depth, glow, grain, and
  motion built into the scene, not from stickers and badges bolted on top.
- **Owner-editable by design.** Every value a non-developer will want to change
  (IP, Discord, version, copy, lists) lives in one commented config file.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text and the bone-on-void palette must hold >=4.5:1;
server data in mono must stay legible. Full keyboard support for nav, FAQ
accordion, and gallery lightbox; visible focus rings (blood-red, not the browser
default). Every animation, including the hero ash/ember motion, has a
`prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` path that drops to a static scene or crossfade.
Live-status colors (online/offline) are paired with text and an icon, never
color alone.
